Sarah has 5 apples. Tom gives her 3 more apples. How many apples does Sarah have now? Sarah now has 8 apples because 5 + 3 = 8.

A store had 24 shirts. They sold 9 shirts today. How many shirts are left? There are 15 shirts left because 24 - 9 = 15.

Each box contains 6 chocolates. If I buy 4 boxes, how many chocolates do I have? You have 24 chocolates because 4 × 6 = 24.

There are 30 students in a class. They need to form teams of 5. How many teams can they make? They can make 6 teams because 30 ÷ 5 = 6.
