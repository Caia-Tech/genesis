What is the chain rule? The chain rule states that d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) * g'(x).
Apply the chain rule to sin(2x). The derivative is 2cos(2x).
What is the product rule? The product rule: d/dx[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x).
Use the product rule on x*sin(x). The derivative is sin(x) + x*cos(x).
What is the quotient rule? The quotient rule: d/dx[f(x)/g(x)] = [f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)]/[g(x)]^2.
Differentiate x/(x+1). Using quotient rule: 1/(x+1)^2.
What is integration by parts? Integration by parts: ∫u dv = uv - ∫v du.
What is L'Hôpital's rule? L'Hôpital's rule helps evaluate limits of indeterminate forms.

What is a partial derivative? A partial derivative is the derivative with respect to one variable while holding others constant.

What is the gradient? The gradient is a vector of all partial derivatives.

What is a differential equation? A differential equation relates a function to its derivatives.
